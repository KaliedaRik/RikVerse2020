It has been eight days since the fact
of the ballot came to roost in the eaves
of our homes; still we disbelieve. Which
neighbour cheered this act? Which sibling?

Those stories we told each other? They are
lies. We are not modern, nor progressive. We
are base; we are dirty. We hate, just as those
who chose otherwise seem to us to loathe the
world beyond our shores. Such a heady new
feeling! It takes our thoughts and mouths, feeds
them a gut-brewed bile so we can poison words -

lazy, witless, unthinking sheep happy to vote
as the headlines command them, keen to cast
that first stone at each new day's enemy ...
this is my future too? Tatters of the dream
that was once my England flap in the gutter;
I will not wait for the next summer storm to wash
the streets clean: I have a brick in my hand.
