Within its clear lattice, a history of hands:
a man in deep places, stroking veins.

An artisan hollows its ice-warm frame.
With the spin of a drill he sees his God

in honeycombs; a bulb forms to pull venom
from poisons. Many hands pass. Poor Eleanor,

a new orphan, gifts it to her prince - her hand
shakes a flake of radiance from its golden flue.

Hands slap to heads as it chimes the floor.
A fresh net of cracks celebrates its gravitas.
